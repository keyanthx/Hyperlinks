Documentation

hyperlinks

It's a symbol of the web's original idea: open, decentralized, no hierarchy, free flow of information. This project is about the hyperlink as a technology as this symbol while both text and visualization use the hyperlink as an inspiration.

text on canvas

I wanted the properties of the hyperlink directly in the visualization and the experience of reading the text. This gave rise to the concept of writing several small texts—what I call fragments—instead of a single large text. Further, I used the nonhierarchical linking of the hyperlink to connect the texts and navigate through them freely. The goal is to create a feeling like digging through the web in your own way.

canvas/space

I call the space where the fragments appear Canvas. The aesthetic of the canvas is kept very simple and refers to Web 1.0. The fragments appear at a random spot on the canvas. This creates an ever-changing spatial connection between the various fragments. Old fragments also disappear after a while. As a result, the canvas is always in motion: new connections form, others dissolve; there is no end. To create a sense of space, I used interactive animations such as the (dis)appearing of the fragments and hover-over effects.

interaction

Interaction is an important component of the project. The hyperlink is at the center of this. It is used for navigation and thereby significantly determines how the texts are experienced. Through animations like hover-overs, I give the fragments additional liveliness. I also use "tooltips," which normally provide a brief preview of links. In this case, I expand upon this technology and use it to display additional information, such as images, thereby expanding the space of the 2D canvas.

technical implementation

For this project, I had code generated using agentic AI for the first time—so-called vibecoding or agentic coding. I quickly realized the many possibilities of this technology. Ideas can be implemented very quickly and at a high technical level. It is not about giving up creative decisions but rather about executing my ideas quickly and with minimal friction.
